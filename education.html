Educational Tips

Learn How to Protect Yourself from QR Code Phishing

What are QR Code Phishing Attacks?

QR code phishing (also known as "quishing") is a type of social engineering attack where cybercriminals use malicious QR codes to trick victims into revealing sensitive information or installing malware.

When scanned, these QR codes redirect users to fake websites that mimic legitimate services, asking for login credentials, payment details, or other personal information.

Common QR Code Attack Vectors

  • Physical tampering - replacing legitimate QR codes with malicious ones
  • Fake advertisements with embedded malicious QR codes
  • QR codes in phishing emails or messages
  • Public charging stations with QR codes that lead to malware

Warning Signs of Malicious QR Codes

  • QR codes that redirect to shortened URLs or suspicious domains
  • Codes that prompt immediate app downloads
  • QR codes that ask for personal information on unsecured websites
  • Stickers placed over original QR codes in public places
  • Unexpected requests for authentication after scanning

Best Practices for QR Code Safety

  • Always check the URL before providing any information
  • Use a QR scanner app that previews URLs before visiting them
  • Be skeptical of QR codes in unexpected places
  • Never enter personal information or credentials after scanning a QR code unless you're certain of its legitimacy
  • Keep your devices and apps updated to protect against vulnerabilities
  • Use VigiQR to verify suspicious QR codes before scanning them with your main device
